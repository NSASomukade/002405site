// The Website

Lost Refuge isn't a tribute to anything in particular, but more of an exploration of how I'd like to express myself when not really given any significant boundaries for how I am to portray myself. Aside from (obviously) the natural, frustrating censorship that comes with mainstream social media, I've spent most of my life up until this point coasting along the ways people expected me to behave, and was reluctant to do anything too 'out of character', as if I was a story someone else was dictating. Now, entering my twenties, I've finally reached a point in my own development through the help of therapy where I feel like I have a better sense of self--one which I'd very much like to explore.

Fancy words aside, this is a website about me, documenting me, because I want something to look back on as I change (the way all things do).

I am seeking a formal education in web-development, but currently I'm only an amateur hobbyiest. Thus, there will likely be broken links, wonky layouts, and inefficient coding. As I said above, though, I'm more than happy to learn and that includes taking criticisms, so please feel free to let me know if and how something could potentially be done better. I am currently only operating on knowledge of HTML and CSS, with no knowledge of JS. Ideally I'd like this website to be accessible to screenreaders and mobile devices, so I'm doing my best to adhere to accessibility guidelines and looking into mobile compatibility.
